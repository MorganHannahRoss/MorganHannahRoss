Core Reflection

Reflection on Foundations Core Skills

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

  • I've learned that I have a tendency to be overly critical of myself, especially when facing challenges or setbacks. Through core learning, I've become more aware of this tendency and have started to practice self-compassion and growth mindset.
  • Core learning has also helped me recognize the importance of active listening and understanding others' perspectives. I've realized that my ego can sometimes get in the way of truly empathizing with others, and I'm working on being more open-minded and empathetic in my interactions.
  • Additionally, I've learned that my values play a significant role in shaping my decisions and actions. Understanding my core values has helped me stay true to myself and make choices aligned with what matters most to me.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

  • Values guide our behavior and decision-making process, ensuring that we prioritize principles such as integrity, collaboration, and continuous improvement. In programming, aligning our actions with our values helps us create ethical and impactful solutions.
  • Empathy allows us to understand the needs and perspectives of users, enabling us to develop user-centric software that meets their requirements effectively. Empathy also fosters collaboration within development teams, leading to better communication and teamwork.
  • Self-awareness helps us recognize our strengths, weaknesses, and biases, allowing us to leverage our strengths and address areas for improvement. In programming, self-awareness enables us to adapt to new technologies, seek help when needed, and continuously grow as developers.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

The most surprising aspect of core learning for me was realizing how much our mindset and emotional intelligence impact our learning and problem-solving abilities. I hadn't fully appreciated the importance of these skills in programming before, but now I see how they can significantly influence our success in the field.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

One of the most challenging aspects of core learning was confronting my own biases and ego-driven behaviors. It's not easy to acknowledge our flaws and vulnerabilities, but doing so is crucial for personal growth and development. Additionally, learning to actively listen and empathize with others, especially when we disagree, required practice and patience.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Programming is not just about writing code; it's also about collaborating with others, understanding user needs, and solving complex problems. Core learning equips us with the interpersonal skills, emotional intelligence, and self-awareness necessary to thrive in the tech industry. Employers are looking for well-rounded individuals who can communicate effectively, work well in teams, and adapt to changing circumstances, making core skills essential for success in a web development Bootcamp course.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, the time spent studying core learning does not feel like a waste of time. While programming skills are important, core learning enhances our ability to communicate, collaborate, and problem-solve effectively, making us more valuable assets in the tech industry. Additionally, developing self-awareness and emotional intelligence improves our overall well-being and resilience, enabling us to navigate challenges and setbacks with greater ease. Therefore, the investment in core learning is essential for our long-term success and fulfillment as developers.
